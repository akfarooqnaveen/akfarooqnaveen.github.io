No Wi-Fi Connection - Model M9110

If you're unable to connect to the Wi-Fi network of your M9110 modem, this document provides troubleshooting steps to help you restore your wireless connection.

Possible Causes

  • Wi-Fi Disabled: The Wi-Fi feature on the modem might be turned off.
  • Incorrect Wi-Fi Settings: Incorrect network name (SSID) or password.
  • Wi-Fi Interference: Interference from other electronic devices or neighboring networks.
  • Outdated Firmware: Older firmware can cause Wi-Fi connectivity issues.
  • Hardware Issue: A problem with the modem's Wi-Fi radio.
  • Router mode disabled on the modem.
  • MAC address filtering.

Troubleshooting Steps

  1. Check Wi-Fi Status: Ensure the Wi-Fi indicator light on your modem is illuminated. If not, check the modem's web interface to enable Wi-Fi.
  2. Verify Wi-Fi Settings: Double-check the network name (SSID) and password. Ensure you are entering them correctly.
  3. Restart Your Modem: Power cycle your modem by unplugging it, waiting 30 seconds, and plugging it back in.
  4. Check for Interference: Move your modem away from other electronic devices that may cause interference (e.g., cordless phones, microwaves). Change the Wi-Fi channel in the modem's settings.
  5. Update Firmware: Check for and install the latest firmware from the manufacturer's website.
  6. Check Router Mode: Ensure that the modem is not in bridge mode, and that router mode is enabled.
  7. Disable MAC Address Filtering: If MAC address filtering is enabled, either disable it or add the MAC address of your device to the allowed list.
  8. Test with Another Device: Try connecting another device to the Wi-Fi network to see if the issue is with a specific device.
  9. Factory Reset: As a last resort, perform a factory reset on your modem. (Note: This will erase all custom settings.)
  10. Contact Your ISP: If the problem persists, contact your ISP for further assistance.
